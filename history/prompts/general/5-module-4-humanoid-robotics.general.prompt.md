# Module 4: Humanoid Robotics

## Prompt Text

Explain the content of Module 4: Humanoid Robotics covering kinematics, balance control, manipulation, HRI design, and conversational robotics.

## Response Text

Module 4 focuses on advanced robotics concepts specific to humanoid robots, including kinematics and balance control, manipulation, human-robot interaction (HRI) design, conversational interfaces, and a capstone project on autonomous humanoid development.

### Humanoid Kinematics and Balance Control
- Understanding the movement and balance mechanisms of bipedal robots
- Key concepts for achieving stable locomotion and posture control
- Integration with perception and planning systems

### Manipulation
- Techniques for humanoid robots to interact with objects in their environment
- Coordination between perception, planning, and actuation systems
- Multi-modal integration for effective manipulation tasks

### Human-Robot Interaction (HRI) Design
- Principles of designing intuitive interaction methods between humans and robots
- Considerations for safety, usability, and acceptance in human environments
- Integration of multiple input/output modalities

### Conversational Robotics
- Natural language interfaces for humanoid robots
- Integration of LLMs with robotic systems for complex task execution
- Contextual understanding and dialogue management in robotic applications

### Capstone: Autonomous Humanoid
- Integration of all concepts learned in previous modules
- Development of an autonomous humanoid robot system
- Focus on real-world application and deployment challenges

## Outcome

Module 4 provides advanced knowledge specific to humanoid robot development, integrating all concepts from previous modules into sophisticated robotic systems.

## Evaluation

This module represents the culmination of the curriculum, preparing students to develop advanced humanoid robots with AI capabilities.
